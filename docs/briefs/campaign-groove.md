# Campaign Groove

## Problem — Progression UX
Level select screen feels like a menu, not a journey. PvZ hides level selection — your account tracks progress and you simply "continue" from where you are. The current 5-level structure with explicit numbered buttons breaks immersion.

## Problem — Audio opportunity
Playtest discovered that Water Pistol firing creates pleasant rhythmic patterns. Jaco's 5-year-old son noticed and started bobbing. Multiple shooters firing at different rates produce emergent musical beats. This is an untapped engagement lever.

## Problem — Replayability
After beating all levels once, there's no reason to return. PvZ had adventure mode replay, survival mode, mini-games, and the zen garden.

## Proposed mechanics

### Campaign auto-progression
Replace explicit level select with a "Continue" button on the title screen. Game tracks progress and loads the next unbeaten level. Completed levels accessible through a map or timeline view for replay, but the primary flow is always forward. Consider a visual world map (bedroom → bathroom → kitchen → garage) for later.

### Audio rhythm enhancement
- Harmonize multi-shooter firing: when multiple Water Pistols fire in the same tick window, quantize to musical intervals
- Add musical motifs to enemy waves (different enemy types trigger different audio textures)
- Victory jingle that builds from the combat sounds
- Potentially: background music that responds to game intensity (adaptive soundtrack)

### Survival / endless mode
Unlocked after beating the campaign. Endless waves with escalating difficulty. High score tracking. Tests how long your defense holds. Good replay value for the "one more try" loop.

## Design questions
- Is the campaign map visual (like PvZ's lawn → night → pool progression) or minimal?
- Should audio enhancement be subtle (background harmony) or prominent (rhythm game hybrid)?
- Does survival mode unlock per-level or once at the end?

## Dependency
Benefits from discovery-journal (gradual introduction) being in place. Can be built independently of it but the experience is better combined.
