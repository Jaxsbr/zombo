# PvZ1 Art and Audio Direction Reference

Textual description of Plants vs. Zombies' visual and audio style, designed to serve as a reference brief for AI-assisted asset generation in Zombo's own aesthetic.

## Visual Style

### Colour palette feel

PvZ uses a **bright, saturated, warm palette** with high contrast between the friendly (player) side and the threatening (zombie) side:

- **Player / plants**: Vivid greens (#4CAF50 range), sunny yellows (#FFC107), warm reds and oranges. Leaves are rich Kelly green, flowers are bold primary colours. Everything reads as healthy, alive, and cheerful.
- **Zombies**: Desaturated grey-greens (#7D8B6A range), sickly yellows, bruise purples. Skin is pale olive-grey with visible green patches of decay. Clothing is muted and tattered. Clearly "unwell" but in a comical, non-frightening way.
- **Environment (Day)**: Bright grass green lawns, clear blue skies, warm sunlight. High-key lighting with soft shadows. Feels like a perfect suburban Saturday morning.
- **Environment (Night)**: Deep blue-purples and cool teals. The lawn darkens to a muted sage. Sky is dark navy with stars. Moon provides ambient cool light. Mushrooms glow softly.
- **Environment (Pool)**: Turquoise-blue water, warm grass. The pool is clean and inviting — suburban backyard pool aesthetic.
- **Environment (Fog)**: Desaturated greens with a milky grey-white fog overlay. Visibility gradient from left (clear) to right (opaque). Eerie but not horror.
- **Environment (Roof)**: Warm terracotta/brown roof tiles, red brick chimney, blue sky. The slope adds visual depth.
- **UI elements**: Warm browns and tans (card-stock/wood texture), green buttons, yellow highlights. The seed packet tray looks like a burlap pouch. Warm, tactile, handcrafted feel.

### Character design language

**Plants:**
- **Shape language**: Round, soft, friendly. Large circular heads/flowers on thin stems. Exaggerated proportions — big eyes, small bodies.
- **Eyes**: Large, expressive, slightly glossy cartoon eyes with black pupils. Positioned front-facing (anthropomorphised). Express personality — Peashooter looks determined, Sunflower looks happy, Chomper looks hungry.
- **Limbs**: Minimal. Most plants have no visible arms/legs — they're rooted. Leaves serve as implied arms for some (Wall-nut has leafy "arms" crossed). Chomper has a stem that lunges.
- **Texture**: Smooth, clean surfaces with subtle cell-shading. No realistic plant textures — stylised, illustrative, almost plasticky.
- **Size**: All plants fit within a single lawn tile (~1:1 aspect ratio). Tall-nut is the tallest, fitting ~2 tiles vertically.

**Zombies:**
- **Shape language**: Lanky, angular, off-balance. Hunched posture, arms extended forward. Intentionally clumsy silhouettes.
- **Eyes**: Small, unfocused, with visible dark circles. Often looking slightly different directions (cross-eyed or vacant). Red or yellow-tinged irises.
- **Clothing**: Suburban dad attire — torn business shirts, loose ties, tattered suits, torn jeans. Implies these were normal people. Football Zombie wears gear, Dancing Zombie has a disco outfit.
- **Colour**: Grey-green skin, varying degrees of decomposition shown through colour patches (not gore). Missing hair patches. Some have visible stitching.
- **Animation pose**: The classic PvZ zombie has arms outstretched, head tilted, shuffling gait. Every zombie variant maintains this "zombie walk" base pose.

### Animation style

- **Frame rate feel**: Smooth but snappy. Movements have clear anticipation-action-follow-through (classic animation principles).
- **Plant attacks**: Quick, punchy. Peashooter's pea-spit is a rapid "pop" with a slight recoil. Cherry Bomb has a brief inflation before explosion. Chomper has a wind-up lunge.
- **Zombie movement**: Slow, rhythmic shuffle. Arms sway. Head bobs. Deliberately comedic — not threatening. When eating, they lean in with exaggerated "nom nom" motions.
- **Hit reactions**: Zombies flash white briefly on hit. Body parts (arm, head, cone) detach cleanly with a small bounce — cartoonish, no blood. Defeated zombies do a brief "collapse" animation.
- **Environmental**: Sun tokens float down with a gentle sine-wave wobble. Lawn mowers activate with a sudden burst. Explosions are bright yellow-orange circles with a comic "BOOM" feel.
- **UI animations**: Seed packets have a grey-out/refill animation for recharge. Sun tokens arc from source to the sun counter. Coins bounce and sparkle.

### UI aesthetic

- **Seed tray**: Brown leather/burlap pouch across the top of the screen. Each seed packet is a small card with the plant illustration, sun cost in a yellow circle, and a grey overlay that recedes as recharge completes.
- **Sun counter**: Top-left, showing a sun icon and number. Warm yellow.
- **Menus**: Wooden plank textures, hand-lettered fonts, warm earth tones. Buttons look tactile (raised, shadowed).
- **Level select**: A map/path with nodes for each level. Themed per world (grass path, stone path, etc.).
- **Font style**: Rounded, slightly irregular, comic-book-style lettering. Not a standard system font — custom, playful, with slight wobble.

## Audio Direction

### Musical mood

The PvZ soundtrack (composed by Laura Shigihara) has a distinctive tone:

- **Overall mood**: Upbeat, quirky, slightly mischievous. Never scary despite the zombie theme. Music makes you smile.
- **Instrumentation**: Primarily acoustic and organic instruments — acoustic guitar, piano, xylophone, marimba, ukulele, light percussion. Some electronic elements in later levels. The "homemade" sound reinforces the suburban backyard setting.
- **Tempo**: Moderate to moderately fast (100-130 BPM typical). Maintains a steady, non-stressful groove even during intense waves.
- **Harmonic feel**: Major keys predominate. Minor passages appear in Night and Fog worlds but remain playful rather than dark. Lots of pentatonic melodies.

### Key tracks

| Track | Context | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **"Grasswalk"** (Main theme) | Day levels (World 1) | Iconic acoustic guitar melody — bouncy, cheerful, instantly recognisable. Sets the tone for the entire game. Fingerpicked guitar with light percussion. |
| **"Moongrains"** | Night levels (World 2) | Mellow, slightly mysterious. Softer dynamics, pizzicato strings, gentle marimba. A lullaby that happens to accompany zombie combat. |
| **"Watery Graves"** | Pool levels (World 3) | Tropical, splashy feel. Steel drums, water-like sound effects woven in. Upbeat and playful despite the name. |
| **"Rigor Mormist"** | Fog levels (World 4) | The most "spooky" track, but still quirky. Low woodwinds, theremin-like synth, muted percussion. Mysterious rather than frightening. |
| **"Graze the Roof"** | Roof levels (World 5) | Energetic, slightly epic. Building intensity to match the final world. More layered instrumentation, driving rhythm. |
| **"Brainiac Maniac"** | Final boss / credits lead-in | Rock-influenced, high-energy. Electric guitar elements appear. The most intense track but still fun, not aggressive. |
| **"Zombies on Your Lawn"** | End credits | A pop song sung (in-fiction) by Sunflower. Catchy, humorous lyrics about zombies wanting brains. Laura Shigihara vocals. Became a viral earworm. |
| **"Loonboon"** | Mini-games | Fast, chaotic, comedic. Reflects the wacky energy of mini-game modes. |

### Sound effect categories

| Category | Examples | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **Plant placement** | Soft "plop" / earth-dig sound when planting. Each plant has a subtle variant. | Earthy, satisfying, organic |
| **Pea/projectile fire** | Quick "pop" or "thwip" for pea shots. Fume-shroom has a breathy "psssh". Lobbers have a chunky "thunk". | Punchy, varied per plant type |
| **Zombie groans** | Low, comedic "braaains" moans. Not frightening — more like a tired person. Multiple variants. | Goofy, non-threatening |
| **Zombie eating** | Crunchy "nom nom" sounds when eating plants. Cartoonish, exaggerated. | Comic, not violent |
| **Explosions** | Cherry Bomb: sharp "BOOM" with a comedic edge. Doom-shroom: deeper, more resonant boom. Jalapeno: fire-whoosh. | Cartoony, impactful but safe |
| **Sun collection** | Bright, twinkling chime when picking up sun. Ascending pitch for multiple collections. | Rewarding, shiny |
| **Coin collection** | Metallic "ding" / "ka-ching" with a sparkle sound. | Classic game reward |
| **UI interaction** | Woody "click" for buttons. Paper shuffle for seed selection. | Tactile, warm |
| **Lawn mower** | Sudden engine rev → mowing sound → zombies being mowed (cartoonish). | Startling but funny |
| **Zombie defeat** | Soft "pop" and body-part bounce. No splat or gore sounds. | Clean, comic |
| **Wave announcements** | Horn/trumpet stinger for "A huge wave is approaching!" Flag Zombie has a tiny fanfare. | Dramatic but playful |
| **Crazy Dave** | Garbled speech ("wabby wabbo") — synthesised/pitch-shifted human voice. | Comedic, endearing |
